Waterco pump performance curves

How to use this at a dealer visit: pace out (or estimate from the site photos) the pipe run and elevation, count the fittings you can see, and read off the required TDH and matched pump directly — no laptop spreadsheet needed.

Pump range covered: Dedicated Solar draws from Supastream (general purpose, low-to-medium head, 40/50mm) first, stepping up to Hydrostorm (50mm, high head) once Supastream can't clear the TDH. Booster (Post-Filter) uses Aquastream MK2 Solar only (40mm) — it's purpose-built for the integrated application, drawing from the filtration line downstream of the filter.

Assumptions: water at ~25°C, uPVC pipe roughness 0.002mm, nominal pipe ID = nominal size. Fitting K‑values and the friction method are taken from Waterco's internal friction-loss reference. Supastream and Hydrostorm curves are fitted from the catalogue's tabulated flow-at-head figures (e.g. flow at 8m/10m), which is more reliable near the duty point but still an extrapolation at the extremes. Aquastream MK2 Solar curves are approximated from the published chart (fitted, anchored to shut-off head and max-flow) since exact tabulated data wasn't published for that range. For a job on the edge between two models, confirm against Waterco's official curve or selection software before quoting.
